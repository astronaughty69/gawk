# GAWK

> A peep show in reverse. She's not performing for you. She's looking at you.

GAWK is an attention training app for adults. Each session pairs a timed image with a micro-lesson on desire, intimacy, and connection — and asks you to stay present with what you're feeling.

**[gawk.pics](https://gawk.pics)** · **[Try the free demo](https://gawk.pics/demo)** — no signup, no credit card

---

## Why This Exists

Screens trained us to scroll past everything — including each other.

Attunement — the ability to read what a partner is actually feeling — requires the opposite: staying present, noticing, being a little uncomfortable with what you find. GAWK is the practice.

> "I just couldn't unsee what scrolling was doing to how we pay attention — and how we relate."

Read the origin story: [Do We Know How to Be Seen?](https://lanaestjohn.com/gawk-peep-show-app/)  
The deeper context: [Substack](https://substack.com/@lanaestjohn)

## What It Does

- Free 14-day trial on signup — no credit card required
- Each session: a micro-lesson on presence or connection, then a timed image to sit with
- Choose your duration: 3s / 5s / 10s / 15s / 30s — longer costs more points
- Points replenish daily
- $9.99/month unlocks all durations and full access

---

## The mechanic

Most apps remove friction. GAWK adds constraint.  
Most apps reward novelty. GAWK allows return.  
Most apps maximize time. GAWK shapes attention.

The timer isn't a punishment. It's the whole point.
